Pizza Margherita

Pizza Margherita originated in Naples, Italy, in 1889, when chef Raffaele Esposito created it in honor of Queen Margherita of Savoy. He used tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil to represent the colors of the Italian flag.

Ingredients for Dough

  • 2¼ tsp (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 1½ cups warm water (110°F / 45°C)
  • 3½ cups all-purpose flour (plus more for kneading)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1½ tsp salt

Ingredients for Toppings

  • Optional: grated parmesan (for extra flavor)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves (a handful)
  • 8 oz (200 g) fresh mozzarella (preferably mozzarella di bufala), torn into pieces
  • Optional: grated parmesan (for extra flavor)

Method

  1. Make dough: Mix yeast, warm water, flour, salt, and olive oil. Let rise 1 hour.
  2. Prepare sauce: Crush canned tomatoes, add salt and olive oil.
  3. Shape dough: Divide and stretch into two pizza bases.
  4. Top: Spread sauce, add mozzarella, basil, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  5. Bake: In a 500°F (260°C) oven for 10 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  6. Serve: Add fresh basil and enjoy hot!
